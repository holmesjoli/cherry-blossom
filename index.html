Cherry blossom blooms are a yearly occurrence, often symbolizing the arrival of spring. Researchers in Kyoto collected data from recorded peak blooms dating back to the 9th century. Peak bloom refers to when 80% or more of the trees' blossoms are open.

Hover over each bloom to see how the data were collected. Data that were collected in the same way will also be highlighted.

Data were collected by researcher Yasuyuki Aono.
